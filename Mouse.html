What Is A Mouse?

A computer mouse is a hand-held pointing device that detects
two-dimensional motion relative to a surface. This motion is typically translated into the motion of a pointer on a
display, which allows a smooth control of the graphical user interface.

History

The first public demonstration of a computer mosue was in
1968. Orginally, computer mice were wired, but some modern mice rely on short-range radio communication between the
mouse and the connected system, usually a computer. Mice used to rely on a rolling ball to detect motion, but these
days have optical sensors, which detect motion, resulting in them not needing any moving parts.
