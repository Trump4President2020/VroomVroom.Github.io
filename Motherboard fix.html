What Is A Motherboard And What Does It Do?

The motherboard,
also sometimes refered to as the main board, baseboard, system board and etc, is the main Printed Circuit Board (PCB) usually used in expandandable systems such as microcomputers. It allocates power and allows communication between all hardware components in the computer such as RAM and the CPU and etc.

History

The first motherboard is thought to have been in the IBM personal
computer which was released in the year 1981. Originally, it was
called "the planar".
